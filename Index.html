U.S. Department of Education Redesign

The U.S. Department of Education website is text-heavy with an uninspiring color palette. I chose to execute a RWD for desktop and mobile.

Research

In this research project, interviews were planned and conducted with a broad range of current and potential users. The questions were structured to find out about user motivations and the usability of the DoE website.

Definition

Users come to the DoE website to learn how to acquire financial aid for college and to manage student loans. Pain-points include a confusing layout and a vibe that feels slightly too cold for an education website.

Ideation

In the initial sketches for the prototypes, I tried to keep simplicity in mind. As the design progressed, I wanted to incorporate a bold color palette and large images to compliment the simpler navigation and add warmth.

Prototyping

The first LoFi versions. User testing could now begin. Different color palettes and the navigation system were at the top of the list of elements to be tested.

Testing

During user testing users were asked about fonts, the color palette, and navigation. Users unanimously felt that a Home button should be added to the pages of the mobile version.

Final Prototype

Based on the data from user testing, a home button was added. Additionally, the font of the copy above the images was increased and made bold, and the images were changed to help with confusion about what page the user was on.
